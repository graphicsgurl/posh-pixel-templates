A slow-burning thing

They met in the back room of a record shop, both reaching for the last pressing of the same album. Sebastian let Vivienne have it on the condition that she let him hear it. That listening lasted until two in the morning, and neither of them has quite stopped since.

Six autumns on, they are marrying by candlelight in an old stone manor — the kind of night that asks for a good coat, a stiff drink, and the people they love most. That is you.

Staying the night

Travel & Stay

The manor is an hour and a half north of the city. We recommend making a weekend of it — the valley is at its most beautiful, and most haunting, in late October.

I.

Getting There

Ninety minutes from Manhattan by car, or the train to Hudson with shuttles to the manor.

II.

Where to Stay

Rooms held at The Maker and the Rhinecliff. Mention the Blackwood–Vane wedding.

III.

Things to Do

Wander the orchards, tour the estate, or find the fireplace and stay by it.

Everything else

Questions

What should I wear?

Black tie with a dark heart — deep jewel tones, moody florals, and anything that looks good by candlelight. This is a night to dress for.

Is there a photobooth?

There is, in the library, all night. Consider it your assignment to leave us at least one incriminating strip.

How cold will it be?

It is late October in the valley — bring a proper coat. The ceremony is indoors and warm; the garden is not.

Are children welcome?

We adore them, but this is a late, adults-only affair — a proper grown-up night out.
