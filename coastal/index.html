Your favourite photo

Two tides, one shore

They met on a crowded ferry from Naples — Isla had lost her ticket to the wind and Matteo, without a word, simply handed the conductor two. By the time the boat reached the coast they had swapped names, numbers, and a very strong opinion about the correct way to eat sfogliatella.

Five summers later, they are returning to the water that introduced them. Nothing would make them happier than to have you there when they say yes.

Making a holiday of it

Travel & Stay

The Amalfi Coast rewards a slow visit. Here are our notes to make the journey easy — where to land, where to rest, and how to spend a few extra days by the sea.

I.

Getting There

Fly into Naples (NAP). From there, a private car or the ferry to Positano — we recommend the ferry for the view.

II.

Where to Stay

Room blocks at Il San Pietro and Le Sirenuse. Mention the Marino–Hart wedding for our rate.

III.

Things to Do

Boat to Capri, hike the Path of the Gods, or do gloriously little. All are correct.

Everything else

Questions

What should I wear?

Coastal cocktail — linens, light colours, and something you can dance in. The terrace is stone, so kind heels are your friend.

Is the ceremony outdoors?

Yes — on an open cliffside terrace above the bay. Bring sunglasses for the ceremony and a light layer for the evening breeze.

Can you help with travel?

Absolutely. Write to us and we will happily share ferry times, drivers, and our favourite places to eat along the coast.

Are children welcome?

We love them, but this will be an adults-only evening so everyone can relax by the water.
